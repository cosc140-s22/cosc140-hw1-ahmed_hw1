The Welsh Senate

The country of Wales has some devolved powers from the UK Government. This allows them control over their cultural, education, health, and the environment to name a few.

These laws are decided by the 60 Member of the Senedd (MS). Even though the vote to give Wales devolved powers was a very narrow decision, many Welsh citizens still strive for more control over their nation such as control over their justice system. But there are also some people in Wales who wish to see greater unity with England.

RUGBY

Rugby is the national sport of Wales. Wales has won Rugby championship games 28 TIMES!

Brought to Wales from England, Rugby has grown to be one of the most popular sport in Wales alongside football. Welsh victories over teir rival nations, including England, has given Wales a spirit of national identity around Rugby.

Castles

Wales is the country of castles. With a long history of war and conquest, from the Normans to the English, castles are an everpresent historic relic in Wales.

Many of these castles and ruins were destroyed over the years but there has been a movement to revie and rebuild Welsh history, and many of these historical sites have seen restoration.
